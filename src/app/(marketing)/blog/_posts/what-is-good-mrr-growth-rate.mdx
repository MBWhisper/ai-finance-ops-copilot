A good MRR growth rate depends entirely on your stage. "10% month-over-month" means something very different at $1K MRR vs. $100K MRR. Here are the 2026 benchmarks every SaaS founder should know.

## The Short Answer

| Stage | MRR Range | Good Monthly Growth Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-revenue / Seed | $0–$10K | 20–30%+ MoM |
| Early traction | $10K–$50K | 15–25% MoM |
| Growth stage | $50K–$200K | 10–20% MoM |
| Scale | $200K–$1M | 5–10% MoM |
| Mature | $1M+ | 3–7% MoM |

The "T2D3" rule (Triple, Triple, Double, Double, Double ARR) is the VC-backed gold standard — but for bootstrapped SaaS, consistent 10–15% MoM at early stage is healthy.

## How to Calculate Your MRR Growth Rate

The formula is simple:

```
MRR Growth Rate = ((MRR This Month - MRR Last Month) / MRR Last Month) × 100
```

**Example:**
- MRR last month: $8,500
- MRR this month: $9,775
- Growth rate: (($9,775 - $8,500) / $8,500) × 100 = **15%**

## Why Raw MRR Growth Is Misleading

A 15% MoM growth rate could hide serious problems. You need to decompose MRR into its components:

- **New MRR** — revenue from brand new customers
- **Expansion MRR** — upgrades and upsells from existing customers
- **Contraction MRR** — downgrades
- **Churned MRR** — lost subscriptions

**Net New MRR = New MRR + Expansion MRR − Contraction MRR − Churned MRR**

A healthy SaaS at growth stage should see expansion MRR offsetting at least 50% of churned MRR. If your churned MRR is higher than expansion, you have a retention problem even if top-line growth looks fine.

## What's Killing Your MRR Growth?

### Problem 1: High Churn Eating New MRR

If you're adding $5K new MRR but losing $4K to churn, you're on a treadmill. Focus on improving activation (first 7-day experience) before spending more on acquisition.

### Problem 2: No Expansion Revenue

Expansion MRR is the most efficient revenue you can generate — zero CAC. If existing customers never upgrade, your pricing tiers may be wrong or your product isn't delivering enough value to justify the next tier.

### Problem 3: Growth Plateau After an Acquisition Spike

Many founders see a spike from a Product Hunt launch, Reddit post, or press mention — then flatline. Sustainable MRR growth comes from repeatable channels (SEO, paid, outbound), not one-off spikes.

## The "Rule of 40" Check

For growth-stage SaaS, the Rule of 40 states:

> **MRR Growth Rate % + Profit Margin % ≥ 40**

If you're growing at 15% MoM but burning cash at -30% margin, you're at 15 + (-30) = -15 — well below the threshold. Investors use this as a quick health check.
