Revenue recognition sounds like an accounting problem — but it directly affects your reported MRR, ARR, and financial health. Get it wrong, and your numbers are meaningless.

## What Is Revenue Recognition?

Revenue recognition is the accounting principle that determines **when** you can count money as revenue. For SaaS, this is especially important because customers often pay upfront for annual plans.

**The key rule:** Revenue is recognized when it's **earned**, not when cash is received.

## The ASC 606 Standard

ASC 606 (Revenue from Contracts with Customers) is the standard that governs how SaaS companies recognize revenue. It follows five steps:

### Step 1: Identify the Contract
- Customer agrees to pay for your service
- Both parties understand the terms
- Payment terms are defined
- The contract has commercial substance

### Step 2: Identify Performance Obligations
What are you delivering? For SaaS:
- Access to the software platform
- Customer support
- Data storage
- Custom integrations (if applicable)

### Step 3: Determine Transaction Price
Total amount the customer will pay:
- Subscription fees
- Setup fees
- Usage-based charges
- Support fees

### Step 4: Allocate Price to Obligations
If you sell multiple things, allocate price to each based on standalone selling price.

**Example:**
- $12,000/year subscription
- $2,000 setup fee
- Standalone price of subscription: $12,000
- Standalone price of setup: $2,000
- Allocation: 85.7% subscription, 14.3% setup

### Step 5: Recognize Revenue When Obligations Are Satisfied

For SaaS subscriptions:
- **Recognize ratably over the subscription period**
- If customer pays $12,000 for 12 months → recognize $1,000/month
- If customer pays $12,000 upfront → $11,000 is deferred revenue initially

## Common SaaS Scenarios

### Annual Subscription (Paid Upfront)
- Customer pays $12,000 on January 1
- 12-month contract
- **Recognition:** $1,000/month for 12 months
- **January balance sheet:** $12,000 cash, $11,000 deferred revenue

### Monthly Subscription
- Customer pays $1,000/month
- **Recognition:** $1,000 each month as earned
- **No deferred revenue** — pay as you go

### Free Trial → Paid
- 14-day free trial, then $99/month
- **Recognition:** $0 during trial, $99/month after conversion

### Setup Fee + Subscription
- $2,000 setup + $500/month
- **Setup fee:** Recognize over expected customer lifetime (typically 24-36 months)
- **Subscription:** Recognize monthly

## Deferred Revenue

**Deferred revenue** = money received but not yet earned. It's a liability on your balance sheet.

**Example:**
- January 1: Customer pays $12,000 for 12 months
- January 1: Deferred revenue = $12,000
- January 31: Deferred revenue = $11,000 (recognized $1,000)
- February 28: Deferred revenue = $10,000
- December 31: Deferred revenue = $0

**Why it matters:**
- Deferred revenue is NOT revenue — it's money you owe (service obligation)
- True MRR = recognized revenue, not cash received
- Investors look at recognized revenue, not cash

## Impact on SaaS Metrics

### MRR Calculation
```
True MRR = Recognized Revenue (not cash received)
```

If you have annual plans:
```
Monthly recognized = Annual payment ÷ 12
```

### ARR Calculation
```
ARR = Recognized annual revenue = MRR × 12
```

### Cash vs. Revenue
| Metric | Cash Basis | Accrual Basis |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| January | $12,000 | $1,000 |
| February | $0 | $1,000 |
| March | $0 | $1,000 |

**Always use accrual basis for SaaS metrics.** Cash basis distorts growth and retention.

## Compliance Checklist

For SaaS founders:

1. **Use accrual accounting** — not cash basis
2. **Track deferred revenue** — every annual plan creates it
3. **Recognize ratably** — over the contract period
4. **Document contracts** — have clear terms
5. **Review quarterly** — ensure recognition matches obligations
6. **Get an accountant** — ASC 606 compliance is complex

## Common Mistakes

1. **Recognizing cash as revenue** — annual plans must be deferred
2. **Ignoring setup fees** — these must be allocated and recognized
3. **Not tracking deferred revenue** — it's a real liability
4. **Mixing up MRR and cash** — they're different numbers
5. **Forgetting usage-based revenue** — recognize when consumed
