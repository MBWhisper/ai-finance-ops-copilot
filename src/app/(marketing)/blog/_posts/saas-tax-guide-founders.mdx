Taxes are the least glamorous part of running a SaaS business — but ignoring them can lead to penalties, audits, and even shutdown. Here's what every founder needs to know.

## SaaS Sales Tax (US)

### The basics

After the 2018 Supreme Court decision (South Dakota v. Wayfair), states can require sales tax collection even if you don't have a physical presence there. The trigger is **economic nexus**.

### Economic Nexus Thresholds

Each state has different thresholds. Common ones:

| State | Threshold | Rate |
|-------|-----------|------|
| California | $500,000 in sales | 7.25% |
| New York | $500,000 in sales | 4%+ |
| Texas | $500,000 in sales | 6.25% |
| Florida | $100,000 in sales | 6% |
| Washington | $100,000 in sales | 6.5% |

**Key point:** The threshold is based on **total sales** to that state, not profit.

### Is SaaS Taxable?

This varies by state:

| State | SaaS Taxable? |
|-------|--------------|
| Texas | Yes |
| California | No (generally) |
| New York | Yes (generally) |
| Florida | No |
| Washington | Yes |
| Illinois | Yes |
| Pennsylvania | Yes |

**The problem:** Rules change frequently. A SaaS product might be taxable in one state and not another.

### What to Do

1. **Track where your customers are** — state-level customer data
2. **Determine nexus** — have you hit thresholds?
3. **Register for sales tax permits** — required before collecting
4. **Collect and remit** — add sales tax to invoices
5. **File returns** — monthly, quarterly, or annually depending on state

## VAT (International)

### EU VAT Rules

If you sell to EU customers:

- **B2C sales** — charge VAT of the customer's country
- **B2B sales** — customer self-accounts (reverse charge)
- **€10,000 threshold** — below this, you can charge your home country rate
- **OSS (One-Stop Shop)** — report all EU VAT in one country

### UK VAT

- Standard rate: 20%
- Register if UK sales exceed £85,000/year
- Similar rules to EU

### Other Countries

Most countries with VAT/GST have similar rules for digital services:
- Australia (GST): A$75,000 threshold
- Canada (GST/HST): C$30,000 threshold
- India (GST): ₹20 lakh threshold
- Japan (JCT): ¥10 million threshold

## Income Tax

### Corporate Tax (US)

- Federal: 21% (flat rate for C-corps)
- State: 0-11.5% (varies by state)
- Pass-through entities: taxed at personal rate

### Corporate Tax (International)

| Country | Rate |

| France | 25% |

### Tax Planning Strategies

1. **Choose the right entity** — LLC, C-corp, S-corp have different tax implications
2. **Track all deductions** — home office, equipment, software, travel
3. **R&D tax credits** — SaaS development often qualifies
4. **State tax planning** — consider where you incorporate
5. **Hire a CPA** — the ROI is enormous

## SaaS-Specific Tax Considerations

### 1. Deferred Revenue and Tax

You might owe tax on cash received before recognizing revenue:
- Customer pays $12,000 in January
- You recognize $1,000/month
- **Tax implications vary** — some states tax on receipt, others on recognition

### 2. Multi-State Nexus

If you have customers in 10+ states, you likely have nexus in several:
- Track customer locations
- Use tax automation tools (TaxJar, Avalara)
- File in each state where you have nexus

### 3. International VAT

Selling globally means VAT compliance:
- Register for VAT in each country above threshold
- Charge correct VAT rates
- File returns (often quarterly)
- Keep records for 5-10 years

## Common Tax Mistakes

1. **Not tracking nexus** — you might owe in states you don't know about
2. **Ignoring international VAT** — EU audits are increasing
3. **Mixing personal and business** — keep finances separate
4. **Not filing on time** — penalties compound
5. **DIY when you shouldn't** — hire a CPA early
