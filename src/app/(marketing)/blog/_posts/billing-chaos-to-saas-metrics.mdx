You're using Stripe for subscriptions, PayPal for one-time payments, and LemonSqueezy for a specific region. Your MRR is somewhere in those three dashboards. But where exactly?

If you're manually adding up numbers from three different platforms to calculate your MRR, churn, and runway -- you're doing it wrong. And you're probably getting it wrong.

## The Problem with Scattered Billing

When billing data lives in multiple places:

- **MRR is guesswork.** Stripe shows $42K, PayPal shows $8K, LemonSqueezy shows $3K. Is your MRR $53K? Not necessarily -- some of those might be one-time payments.
- **Churn is invisible.** You can see cancellations in Stripe, but what about PayPal refunds? LemonSqueezy downgrades? They don't talk to each other.
- **Forecasting is unreliable.** Your cash flow forecast only works if it includes all revenue sources. A forecast based on Stripe alone misses 15% of your actual revenue.

## The Unification Approach

Here's how to consolidate your billing data in one week:

### Day 1-2: Audit Your Sources

List every payment platform you use:
- Stripe (subscriptions)
- PayPal (one-time or subscriptions)
- LemonSqueezy (subscriptions or digital products)
- Any other processor (Paddle, Gumroad, etc.)

For each, note:
- What type of payments it handles
- How much revenue it generates monthly
- Whether it has an API or webhook support

### Day 3-4: Connect to a Unified Data Layer

You have two options:

**Option A: Use AI Finance Ops**
Connect your Stripe, PayPal, and LemonSqueezy accounts directly. AI Finance Ops automatically:
- Identifies recurring vs. one-time payments
- Calculates true MRR across all platforms
- Tracks churn regardless of where it happens
- Forecasts cash flow from all revenue sources

**Option B: Build a Manual Pipeline**
If you prefer DIY:
1. Export monthly data from each platform (CSV)
2. Import into a spreadsheet or database
3. Manually categorize each transaction (recurring vs. one-time)
4. Calculate MRR, churn, and growth rate

### Day 5-6: Validate Your Numbers

Compare the unified numbers against each individual platform:
- Total MRR should match the sum of platform-specific MRR (minus one-time payments)
- Churn should account for cancellations on ALL platforms
- Growth rate should reflect net change across all sources

### Day 7: Set Up Ongoing Tracking

Choose one of:
- Automated sync (AI Finance Ops or custom webhook pipeline)
- Weekly manual export and import
- Monthly reconciliation

## What Clean Metrics Look Like

Once unified, you can answer:
- **True MRR:** $53,200 (not "around $50K from Stripe plus some from PayPal")
- **Real churn rate:** 4.2% (not "Stripe says 3% but we lost some PayPal customers too")
- **Actual growth:** +8.5% MoM (not "Stripe grew 10% but PayPal shrank")
