Whether you're selling your SaaS or raising a round, due diligence is coming. The better prepared you are, the faster and smoother the process — and the higher your valuation.

## What Is Due Diligence?

Due diligence is when an acquirer or investor examines every aspect of your business to verify claims and assess risk. It typically takes 4-8 weeks and covers:

1. **Financial** — revenue, expenses, unit economics
2. **Legal** — contracts, IP, compliance
3. **Technical** — code, infrastructure, security
4. **Operational** — team, processes, customers

## Financial Due Diligence

This is where most deals are made or broken.

### Metrics They'll Ask For

| Metric | What They Want |
|--------|---------------|
| MRR/ARR | 12-24 months of history |
| MRR Growth | Month-over-month trend |
| Churn | Monthly logo and revenue churn |
| LTV:CAC | Unit economics health |
| Gross Margin | Revenue minus COGS |
| Net Revenue Retention | Expansion vs. contraction |
| Burn Rate | Monthly cash consumption |
| Runway | Months until cash runs out |

### Documents to Prepare

1. **P&L statements** — 2-3 years of monthly financials
2. **Bank statements** — last 12 months
3. **Stripe/payment processor reports** — full transaction history
4. **Tax returns** — last 2-3 years
5. **Cap table** — who owns what
6. **Budget and forecasts** — forward-looking projections

### Financial Red Flags

- Inconsistent revenue recognition
- High churn hidden by new customer growth
- Revenue concentration (>20% from one customer)
- Unexplained expenses
- Cash vs. accrual discrepancies

## Legal Due Diligence

### Contracts to Review

1. **Customer agreements** — Terms of Service, SLAs
2. **Vendor contracts** — hosting, tools, services
3. **Employment agreements** — NDAs, non-competes
4. **IP assignments** — code ownership
5. **Investor agreements** — existing term sheets

### Legal Checklist

- [ ] All code is owned by the company (no contractor IP issues)
- [ ] Customer data handling complies with GDPR/CCPA
- [ ] No pending lawsuits or disputes
- [ ] Trademarks and domains are registered
- [ ] Insurance policies are current

## Technical Due Diligence

### Code Quality

- [ ] Clean, documented codebase
- [ ] Test coverage above 70%
- [ ] No critical security vulnerabilities
- [ ] Version control with clear history
- [ ] CI/CD pipeline in place

### Infrastructure

- [ ] Scalable architecture
- [ ] Monitoring and alerting
- [ ] Backup and disaster recovery
- [ ] Uptime above 99.5%
- [ ] No single points of failure

### Security

- [ ] Data encryption at rest and in transit
- [ ] SOC 2 compliance (or plan for it)
- [ ] Regular security audits
- [ ] Access controls and audit logs
- [ ] Incident response plan

## Operational Due Diligence

### Team

- Key person risk (can the business run without you?)
- Employee contracts and retention
- Team size vs. revenue efficiency

### Customers

- Customer concentration
- Contract lengths and renewal rates
- Net Promoter Score (NPS)
- Support ticket volume and resolution time

### Processes

- Documentation quality
- Onboarding process
- Deployment process
- Monitoring and incident response

## Preparation Timeline

### 6 Months Before
- Clean up financials
- Organize legal documents
- Fix technical debt
- Reduce customer concentration

### 3 Months Before
- Audit all contracts
- Document all processes
- Prepare data room
- Get a quality of earnings report

### 1 Month Before
- Final metrics review
- Prepare management presentation
- Anticipate questions
- Line up references

## Valuation Multiples

SaaS companies typically sell for:

| Stage | ARR Multiple |
|-------|-------------|
| Pre-revenue | 2-5x ARR |
| $1M-$5M ARR | 3-6x ARR |
| $5M-$20M ARR | 5-10x ARR |
| $20M+ ARR | 8-15x ARR |

**Factors that increase valuation:**
- High NRR (above 120%)
- Low churn (below 3% monthly)
- Strong growth (above 50% YoY)
- High gross margins (above 80%)
- Recurring revenue (above 90% of total)

**Factors that decrease valuation:**
- High customer concentration
- Key person dependency
- Technical debt
- Legal issues
- Inconsistent metrics
