You can have a great product and still lose an investor in the first five minutes of a meeting by not knowing your numbers. Here are the 9 metrics investors check — and what good looks like for each.

## 1. MRR and MRR Growth Rate

Monthly Recurring Revenue is the foundation. But investors care more about the **growth rate** than the absolute number.

- **What's good:** 10–20% MoM at early stage, 5–10% at growth stage
- **Red flag:** MRR growth slowing below 5% before $100K ARR
- **What they'll ask:** "Walk me through your MRR decomposition."

## 2. Net Revenue Retention (NRR)

NRR measures how much revenue you keep from existing customers after expansions, contractions, and churn.

```
NRR = (Starting MRR + Expansion MRR − Contraction MRR − Churned MRR) / Starting MRR × 100
```

- **World-class:** >120% (customers spend more every year)
- **Good:** 100–120%
- **Concerning:** <100% (you're losing more than you're expanding)

NRR above 100% means your existing customers alone will grow your revenue even if you sign zero new customers.

## 3. Customer Churn Rate

Monthly customer churn tells investors how "leaky" your bucket is.

- **B2B SaaS benchmark:** <2% monthly churn
- **Consumer SaaS:** <5% monthly churn
- **Red flag:** >5% monthly for B2B — means average customer life under 20 months

## 4. CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)

How much do you spend to acquire one paying customer?

```
CAC = Total Sales & Marketing Spend / New Customers Acquired
```

Investors want to see CAC trending down as you scale (efficiency improving) and always below LTV.

## 5. LTV:CAC Ratio

The single most important unit economics metric.

- **Minimum viable:** 3:1
- **Healthy:** 5:1+
- **Too high (>10:1):** You're under-investing in growth

A 3:1 LTV:CAC means for every $1 you spend acquiring a customer, you get $3 back over their lifetime.

## 6. Payback Period

How many months to recover your CAC from a customer's gross margin contribution?

- **SaaS benchmark:** 12–18 months
- **Best-in-class:** Under 12 months
- **Red flag:** Over 24 months — cash inefficiency kills growth

## 7. Gross Margin

SaaS gross margins should be high — that's the whole business model.

- **Benchmark:** 70–85% gross margin
- **Red flag:** Below 60% — suggests high infrastructure cost or services-heavy delivery
- **Infrastructure/AI tools:** 60–70% is acceptable given compute costs

## 8. ARR per Employee

An efficiency metric that shows how scalable the business is.

- **Early stage:** $100K–$150K ARR/employee
- **Growth stage:** $200K+ ARR/employee
- **Top quartile:** $500K+ ARR/employee

## 9. Rule of 40

Growth rate + profit margin ≥ 40.

At early stage, most investors relax this. But by Series A, you should be tracking it and have a clear path to hitting it by Series B.

## How to Present These Numbers

Prepare a single-page metrics snapshot before any investor call:

1. Current MRR + MoM growth rate (last 6 months)
2. NRR
3. Customer count + monthly churn %
4. CAC, LTV, LTV:CAC ratio
5. Payback period
6. Gross margin %
7. Burn rate + runway (months)
