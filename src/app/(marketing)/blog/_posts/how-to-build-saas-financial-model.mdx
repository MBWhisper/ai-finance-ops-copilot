A SaaS financial model helps you forecast revenue, plan expenses, and make data-driven decisions. It's essential for fundraising, budgeting, and strategic planning.

## What Is a SaaS Financial Model?

A financial model is a spreadsheet (or tool) that projects your business's financial performance over time. For SaaS, it typically covers:

- Revenue projections (MRR/ARR growth)
- Expense forecasting (team, tools, marketing)
- Cash flow planning (when you'll be profitable or need funding)
- Scenario analysis (best case, worst case, base case)

## Step 1: Define Your Assumptions

Every financial model starts with assumptions. Be realistic.

### Revenue Assumptions
- Current MRR
- Monthly growth rate
- Churn rate
- Expansion rate
- Pricing per tier

### Expense Assumptions
- Team size and salaries
- Tools and infrastructure costs
- Marketing spend
- Office/remote costs
- Contractor and service costs

### Growth Assumptions
- New customer acquisition rate
- Trial-to-paid conversion rate
- Average revenue per account (ARPA)

## Step 2: Build the Revenue Model

### Monthly Revenue Projection

```
Month 1 MRR: Starting MRR
Month 2 MRR: Month 1 MRR × (1 + Growth Rate) - Churned MRR + Expansion MRR
```

### 12-Month Revenue Forecast

| Month | Starting MRR | New MRR | Expansion | Churn | Ending MRR |
|-------|-------------|---------|-----------|-------|------------|
| Jan | $10,000 | +$2,000 | +$500 | -$500 | $12,000 |
| Feb | $12,000 | +$2,400 | +$600 | -$600 | $14,400 |
| Mar | $14,400 | +$2,880 | +$720 | -$720 | $17,280 |

**Key formulas:**
- New MRR = Previous MRR × Growth Rate
- Expansion MRR = Previous MRR × Expansion Rate (typically 2-5%)
- Churned MRR = Previous MRR × Churn Rate

## Step 4: Cash Flow Projection

```
Monthly Cash Flow = Revenue - Expenses
Cumulative Cash = Starting Cash + Sum of Monthly Cash Flows
```

| Month | Revenue | Expenses | Cash Flow | Cumulative |
|-------|---------|----------|-----------|------------|
| Jan | $12,000 | $18,000 | -$6,000 | $194,000 |
| Feb | $14,400 | $19,000 | -$4,600 | $189,400 |
| Mar | $17,280 | $20,000 | -$2,720 | $186,680 |

## Step 5: Scenario Analysis

Always model three scenarios:

### Base Case
- Growth rate: 8%/month
- Churn: 4%/month
- Expenses grow 5%/month

### Best Case
- Growth rate: 12%/month
- Churn: 3%/month
- Expenses grow 3%/month

### Worst Case
- Growth rate: 3%/month
- Churn: 6%/month
- Expenses grow 8%/month

## Step 6: Key Metrics to Track

Your model should automatically calculate:

1. **MRR and ARR** — revenue growth trajectory
2. **Runway** — months until cash runs out
3. **Break-even date** — when revenue covers expenses
4. **LTV:CAC** — unit economics health
5. **Burn multiple** — spending efficiency

## Common Modeling Mistakes

1. **Over-optimistic growth** — use industry benchmarks, not dreams
2. **Forgetting churn** — churn compounds over time
3. **Ignoring seasonality** — Q4 might be 2x Q1
4. **Not updating monthly** — a model is only useful if current
5. **Single scenario** — always model best/worst/base
