Unit economics tell you whether your SaaS business model actually works — before you scale. If your unit economics are broken, more customers just means more losses.

This guide covers every metric you need, how to calculate them, and what "good" looks like.

## What Are SaaS Unit Economics?

Unit economics measure the direct revenue and costs associated with acquiring and serving a single customer. The two core metrics are:

- **Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)** — how much revenue one customer generates over their lifetime
- **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)** — how much it costs to acquire one customer

The relationship between these two numbers determines whether your business is viable.

## LTV:CAC Ratio

The golden rule of SaaS unit economics:

```
LTV:CAC Ratio = Customer Lifetime Value ÷ Customer Acquisition Cost
```

| Ratio | Verdict |
|-------|---------|
| < 1:1 | Losing money on every customer |
| 1:1 – 2:1 | Unsustainable — growth kills you |
| 3:1 | Healthy — industry benchmark |
| 5:1+ | Excellent — room to invest aggressively |
| > 7:1 | Under-investing in growth |

**Target: 3:1 or higher.** For every $1 spent on acquisition, you should get $3+ back in lifetime revenue.

## How to Calculate LTV

The simplest formula:

```
LTV = ARPA × Gross Margin ÷ Monthly Churn Rate
```

**Example:**
- ARPA (Average Revenue Per Account): $100/month
- Gross Margin: 80%
- Monthly Churn Rate: 5%

```
LTV = $100 × 0.80 ÷ 0.05 = $1,600
```

### LTV Variations

**Simple LTV:**
```
LTV = ARPA ÷ Churn Rate
```

**Adjusted LTV (with expansion revenue):**
```
LTV = (ARPA + Expansion MRR per Customer) ÷ Churn Rate × Gross Margin
```

**Cohort-Based LTV (most accurate):**
Track actual revenue per cohort over 12-24 months rather than using a formula.

## How to Calculate CAC

```
CAC = Total Sales & Marketing Spend ÷ Number of New Customers Acquired
```

**Include ALL costs:**
- Ad spend (Google, LinkedIn, Facebook)
- Content marketing costs
- Sales team salaries + commissions
- Marketing tools and software
- Events and sponsorships
- Creative and design costs

**Example:**
- Monthly marketing spend: $10,000
- Sales team cost: $15,000
- Tools: $2,000
- New customers: 50

```
CAC = ($10,000 + $15,000 + $2,000) ÷ 50 = $540
```

### Blended vs. Paid CAC

- **Blended CAC** — total spend ÷ all new customers (includes organic)
- **Paid CAC** — only paid channels ÷ customers from paid channels

Always track both. Paid CAC shows your true acquisition cost; blended shows overall efficiency.

## Payback Period

How long it takes to recover your CAC:

```
Payback Period (months) = CAC ÷ (ARPA × Gross Margin)
```

**Example:**
- CAC: $540
- ARPA: $100/month
- Gross Margin: 80%

```
Payback = $540 ÷ ($100 × 0.80) = 6.75 months
```

| Payback Period | Assessment |
|----------------|-----------|
| < 6 months | Excellent — fast payback |
| 6-12 months | Good — industry standard |
| 12-18 months | Acceptable for enterprise SaaS |
| > 18 months | Risky — ties up capital |

**Shorter payback = faster growth.** If you recover CAC in 6 months, you can reinvest that money 2x per year.

## Gross Margin

```
Gross Margin = (Revenue - COGS) ÷ Revenue × 100
```

**What counts as COGS for SaaS:**
- Hosting infrastructure (AWS, Vercel, Supabase)
- Third-party API costs (Stripe, payment processing)
- Customer support costs (if tied to delivery)
- CDN and bandwidth costs

**Typical SaaS gross margins:**
- Self-serve SaaS: 75-85%
- Enterprise SaaS: 70-80%
- Infrastructure SaaS: 60-75%

**Target: 80%+ gross margin.** Below 70% makes it hard to fund growth.

## Quick Ratio (SaaS Growth Efficiency)

```
Quick Ratio = (New MRR + Expansion MRR) ÷ (Churned MRR + Contraction MRR)
```

| Quick Ratio | Growth Assessment |
|-------------|------------------|
| < 1 | Shrinking |
| 1-2 | Slow growth |
| 2-4 | Healthy growth |
| 4+ | Exceptional growth |

**Example:**
- New MRR: $5,000
- Expansion MRR: $2,000
- Churned MRR: $1,500
- Contraction MRR: $500

```
Quick Ratio = ($5,000 + $2,000) ÷ ($1,500 + $500) = 3.5
```

## Magic Number (Sales Efficiency)

```
Magic Number = Net New ARR (current quarter) ÷ Sales & Marketing Spend (previous quarter)
```

| Magic Number | Assessment |
|-------------|-----------|
| < 0.75 | Inefficient — fix before scaling |
| 0.75-1.0 | Good — invest more in sales |
| 1.0+ | Excellent — high sales efficiency |

## Putting It All Together: A Complete Example

**Company: SaaS Startup**
- MRR: $50,000
- Customers: 500
- ARPA: $100/month
- Monthly churn: 3%
- Gross margin: 80%
- Monthly CAC: $400
- Sales & Marketing spend: $20,000/month
- New customers: 50/month

**Calculated Metrics:**
- LTV = $100 × 0.80 ÷ 0.03 = **$2,667**
- LTV:CAC = $2,667 ÷ $400 = **6.7:1** (excellent)
- Payback = $400 ÷ $80 = **5 months** (excellent)
- Quick Ratio = ($5,000 + $1,000) ÷ ($1,500 + $500) = **3.0** (healthy)

## How to Improve Your Unit Economics

### Increase LTV
1. **Reduce churn** — improve onboarding, add activation emails
2. **Expand revenue** — upsell, cross-sell, usage-based pricing
3. **Raise prices** — most SaaS undercharges by 20-50%

### Reduce CAC
1. **Improve conversion rates** — optimize landing pages, trial-to-paid flow
2. **Invest in content/SEO** — lower CAC over time
3. **Refine targeting** — focus on ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
4. **Leverage referrals** — existing customers bring new ones at near-zero CAC

### Improve Gross Margin
1. **Optimize infrastructure** — right-size servers, use spot instances
2. **Negotiate vendor pricing** — volume discounts on APIs
3. **Automate support** — chatbots, knowledge base, self-serve

## Common Mistakes

1. **Ignoring churn in LTV** — a 5% monthly churn means average customer stays only 20 months
2. **Using revenue instead of gross margin** — LTV should be based on profit, not revenue
3. **Not including all CAC costs** — salaries, tools, events all count
4. **Comparing to wrong benchmarks** — B2C SaaS ≠ Enterprise SaaS benchmarks
5. **Tracking blended only** — always separate organic vs. paid CAC

## Benchmark Summary

| Metric | Poor | Average | Good | Excellent |
|--------|------|---------|------|-----------|
| LTV:CAC | < 1 | 1-2 | 3-5 | 5+ |
| Payback | > 18mo | 12-18mo | 6-12mo | < 6mo |
