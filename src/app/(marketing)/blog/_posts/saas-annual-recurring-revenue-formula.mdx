ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue) is the most cited metric in SaaS — but it's also one of the most frequently miscalculated. Here's the exact formula, worked examples, and the mistakes that skew your numbers.

## The ARR Formula

```
ARR = MRR × 12
```

That's it. ARR is simply your Monthly Recurring Revenue annualized.

**Example:**
- January MRR: $12,500
- ARR: $12,500 × 12 = **$150,000**

## Why ARR ≠ Annual Revenue

This is the most important distinction. ARR is a **forward-looking metric** based on current subscriptions. Annual revenue is a **backward-looking accounting metric** based on cash received.

| Metric | What It Measures | Time Orientation |
|---|---|---|
| ARR | Current run rate, annualized | Forward-looking |
| Annual Revenue | Cash actually received in a year | Backward-looking |
| Bookings | Value of new contracts signed | Point-in-time |

If you have $150K ARR in January, it means your current subscribers would generate $150K *if nothing changes for 12 months*. It does NOT mean you received $150K last year.

## ARR for Different Subscription Types

### Monthly Subscriptions
```
Customer A: $99/month → $1,188 ARR
Customer B: $299/month → $3,588 ARR
Total ARR = $4,776
```

### Annual Subscriptions
For annual plans, do NOT multiply by 12 — you'd be double-counting.
```
Customer C pays $2,400/year upfront → $2,400 ARR (already annual)
```

### Mixed Portfolio
```
10 customers × $99/month = $990 MRR → $11,880 ARR from monthly
5 customers × $1,200/year = $6,000 ARR from annual
Total ARR = $11,880 + $6,000 = $17,880
```

## The 5 Most Common ARR Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Including One-Time Revenue
Setup fees, consulting, custom development — these are not recurring. Exclude them from ARR.

### Mistake 2: Using Gross Revenue Instead of Net
If you have a 10% payment processor fee, your ARR should be based on net revenue after processor fees — unless you're calculating gross ARR for investor reporting (always clarify which).

### Mistake 3: Not Normalizing Annual Plans
If a customer cancels their annual plan in month 3, you don't keep all 12 months of ARR. Adjust ARR downward when customers churn, even on annual plans.

### Mistake 4: Including Trials and Freemium Users
ARR is **recurring revenue only**. Free tier users, trial users, and expired subscriptions do not count.

### Mistake 5: Snapshoting ARR on the Wrong Date
ARR is a point-in-time metric. Measure it at the same time each month (e.g., last day of month) for consistent trends.

## ARR vs MRR: When to Use Each

- **Use MRR** for operational decisions — month-to-month growth, churn analysis, forecasting
- **Use ARR** for investor reporting, benchmarking, company valuation, press releases

Investors quote ARR because it's a larger, more impressive number. Internally, MRR is more useful for decisions.

## ARR-Based Valuation

Early-stage SaaS companies are often valued at a multiple of ARR:

- **Bootstrapped / pre-traction:** 2–4× ARR
- **Growing (20%+ MoM):** 5–10× ARR
- **VC-backed, high-growth:** 10–30× ARR

At $150K ARR with 15% MoM growth, a rough valuation range might be $750K–$1.5M.
