Most SaaS founders build one runway projection and call it a plan. Then reality hits: churn spikes, a big customer delays payment, or growth slows for two months. Suddenly the "12-month runway" is actually 7 months.

Scenario planning fixes this by showing you three versions of the future — best case, base case, and worst case — so you're never caught off guard.

## Why One Number Isn't Enough

Your runway depends on assumptions that are all uncertain:
- **Revenue growth rate** — Will you grow 10% or 20% month over month?
- **Churn rate** — Will it stay at 3% or jump to 6%?
- **Burn rate** — Will expenses stay flat or increase with hiring?

When you build a single projection, you're picking one value for each variable. But the real world doesn't pick one value — it picks whatever it wants.

## The Three-Scenario Model

Here's how to structure your runway model:

### Best Case (P95)
Everything goes right. New cohorts convert well, churn drops, and you land a big customer.

| Month | Starting Cash | Revenue | Expenses | Net Burn | Ending Cash |
|-------|--------------|---------|----------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | $500,000 | $50,000 | $35,000 | -$0 | $500,000 |
| 2 | $500,000 | $57,500 | $35,000 | +$22,500 | $522,500 |
| 3 | $522,500 | $66,125 | $36,000 | +$30,125 | $552,625 |

**Runway:** Infinite — you're profitable by month 2.

### Base Case (P50)
Things go roughly as expected. Growth is steady, churn is normal.

| Month | Starting Cash | Revenue | Expenses | Net Burn | Ending Cash |
|-------|--------------|---------|----------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | $500,000 | $40,000 | $35,000 | -$0 | $500,000 |
| 2 | $500,000 | $44,000 | $35,000 | +$9,000 | $509,000 |
| 3 | $509,000 | $48,400 | $36,000 | +$12,400 | $521,400 |

**Runway:** 18+ months — comfortable.

### Worst Case (P50)
Growth stalls, churn doubles, and you lose a customer.

| Month | Starting Cash | Revenue | Expenses | Net Burn | Ending Cash |
|-------|--------------|---------|----------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | $500,000 | $35,000 | $35,000 | -$0 | $500,000 |
| 2 | $500,000 | $33,250 | $36,000 | -$2,750 | $497,250 |
| 3 | $497,250 | $31,588 | $36,000 | -$4,412 | $492,838 |

**Runway:** 14 months — still okay, but you need to act.

## How to Set Your Assumptions

For each scenario, set values for:

1. **MRR growth rate** — Best: +15%, Base: +8%, Worst: -5%
2. **Churn rate** — Best: 2%, Base: 4%, Worst: 8%
3. **Expense growth** — Best: flat, Base: +3%/month, Worst: +5%/month
4. **One-time events** — Best: land big customer, Base: none, Worst: lose a customer

## The P50/P80/P95 Framework

Investors think in percentiles:
- **P50** = 50% chance you hit this (base case)
- **P80** = 80% chance you hit this (conservative)
- **P95** = 95% chance you hit this (things go wrong)

Your fundraising timeline should be based on P80 or P95, not P50. If your P80 runway is 12 months, start fundraising at month 6.
