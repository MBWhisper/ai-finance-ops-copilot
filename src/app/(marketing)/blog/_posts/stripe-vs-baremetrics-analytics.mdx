Stripe now has a surprisingly capable analytics dashboard. So is Baremetrics still worth $150–$400/month? The answer depends on what stage you're at and what you actually need.

## What Stripe's Native Analytics Gives You

Stripe has improved its built-in reporting significantly. In 2026, you get:

- **Revenue overview** — gross revenue, net revenue, refunds
- **MRR and ARR** — visible in the dashboard for subscription products
- **Churn** — customer and revenue churn visible under Subscriptions
- **Active subscribers** — count and trend
- **Failed payment recovery** — Smart Retries and Revenue Recovery stats
- **Cohort analysis** — basic retention by cohort

For a founder at $0–$5K MRR, this is often enough.

Baremetrics shines when you need **decomposed MRR visibility** — understanding exactly how much of your growth is new customers vs expansions vs recovering churned accounts. Stripe bundles these together.

## The Real Difference: Actionability

Stripe tells you *what happened*. Baremetrics helps you understand *why* and *what to do next*.

Example: Stripe shows your MRR dropped by $2,000 this month. Baremetrics tells you it dropped because 4 customers churned (−$3,200) but 2 upgraded (+$1,200), giving you a net −$2,000. Those are two completely different operational responses.

## When Stripe Native Is Enough

- You're under $10K MRR
- You have one product, one price point
- You don't need forecasting or runway visibility
- You can check Stripe daily yourself

## When You Need More Than Stripe

- You're above $10K MRR and decisions require precise metric decomposition
- You need to share metrics with co-founders, investors, or a board
- You want email/Slack digests without logging into Stripe
- You're tracking trial-to-paid conversion as a core KPI

## The $129/Month Problem

Baremetrics starts at $129/month for basic features. At $5K MRR, that's **2.6% of your revenue** going to a metrics tool. For bootstrapped founders, that's hard to justify.
