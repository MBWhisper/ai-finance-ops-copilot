Stripe makes MRR tracking easy. But millions of SaaS products use PayPal, Gumroad, Paddle, or LemonSqueezy — and many early-stage founders bill manually. Here's how to track MRR accurately regardless of your payment processor.

## Why MRR Tracking Is Harder Without Stripe

Stripe was built with subscriptions as a first-class feature. Every charge is tagged with a subscription ID, interval, and customer. This makes MRR calculation straightforward.

Other processors treat subscriptions differently:

- **PayPal** — Subscription billing exists but API data is inconsistent
- **Gumroad** — Memberships are supported but no native MRR calculation
- **Paddle** — Good subscription support, but MRR dashboard is limited on lower plans
- **LemonSqueezy** — Growing subscription feature set, but newer
- **Manual billing** — Wire transfers, invoices — no automated tracking at all

## Method 1: Manual Spreadsheet (Works, But Slow)

The simplest approach for under 20 customers:

1. Create a spreadsheet with columns: Customer, Plan, Monthly Amount, Start Date, Status
2. Each month, sum all Active rows under Monthly Amount
3. Track changes manually: new customers (+), upgrades (+), downgrades (-), cancellations (-)

**MRR = SUM of all active subscription monthly amounts**

For annual plans, divide by 12 to normalize to monthly:
```
Annual plan $1,200/year → $100 MRR contribution
```

**Limitation:** Takes 30–60 min/month. Error-prone. No decomposition.

## Method 2: Use Your Processor's Export + CSV Import

Most processors let you export subscription data as CSV:

- **PayPal:** Reports → Statements → Download
- **Gumroad:** Analytics → Export (memberships)
- **Paddle:** Reports → Subscriptions → Export
- **LemonSqueezy:** Reports → Subscriptions

Once you have the CSV, you can paste it into a tracking tool that accepts manual imports.

## Method 4: Normalize Everything to Monthly

The most common MRR calculation mistake is double-counting annual plans.

**Wrong:** A customer pays $1,200 upfront. You record $1,200 MRR in January.
**Right:** Divide by 12. Record $100 MRR each month they're active.

Same applies to quarterly plans:
```
Quarterly plan $300/quarter → $100 MRR contribution
```

## Handling Failed Payments and Pauses

- **Failed payment:** Do not count this subscription toward MRR until payment succeeds
- **Paused subscription:** Exclude from MRR during the pause period
- **Refunded month:** Deduct from the month the refund occurred

## The One Number That Matters

Regardless of your processor, the metric you care about is **Net New MRR**:

```
Net New MRR = New MRR + Expansion MRR − Contraction MRR − Churned MRR
```

If this number is positive every month, your business is growing. If it's negative, you're contracting even if total MRR looks stable (churn is eating new customers).
