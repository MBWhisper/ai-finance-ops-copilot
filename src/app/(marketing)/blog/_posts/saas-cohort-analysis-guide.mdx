Cohort analysis is the most powerful tool for understanding customer behavior. It groups customers by when they signed up and tracks how each group behaves over time.

## What Is Cohort Analysis?

A **cohort** is a group of customers who share a common characteristic — typically their signup month. Cohort analysis tracks these groups over time to reveal patterns that aggregate data hides.

**Example:**
- January cohort: 100 customers, 80% retention after 3 months
- February cohort: 120 customers, 65% retention after 3 months
- March cohort: 90 customers, 70% retention after 3 months

The February cohort performed worse — maybe a bad onboarding flow or a pricing change. Cohort analysis reveals this; aggregate data would miss it.

## Why Cohort Analysis Matters

### 1. Reveals True Retention

Aggregate retention can be misleading. If you acquire 100 customers in January and 200 in February, overall retention looks good — but the February cohort might be churning faster.

### 2. Identifies Churn Patterns

Cohort analysis shows exactly when customers churn:
- Day 1-7: Onboarding failure
- Month 1-3: Value not delivered
- Month 3-6: Competitor switch
- Month 6+: Budget cuts or need change

### 3. Measures Feature Impact

Launch a new feature in March? Compare the March cohort to February — did retention improve?

### 4. Improves Forecasting

Cohort-based revenue forecasts are far more accurate than simple growth projections.

## How to Do Cohort Analysis

### Step 1: Define Your Cohort

The most common cohort definition:
- **Monthly signup cohort** — group by month of signup
- **Weekly signup cohort** — for high-volume products
- **Annual cohort** — for enterprise SaaS

### Step 2: Choose Your Metric

Track one of these per cohort:
- **Retention rate** — % of customers still active
- **Revenue retention** — % of MRR still active
- **NPS score** — satisfaction over time
- **Feature usage** — engagement trends

### Step 4: Visualize with a Retention Curve

Plot retention curves for each cohort. Look for:
- **Diverging curves** — later cohorts retaining better (good!)
- **Converging curves** — all cohorts behave the same (predictable)
- **Steep drops** — specific points where customers churn

## Key Cohort Metrics

### Gross Revenue Retention (GRR)

Revenue retained from existing customers (excluding expansion):

```
GRR = (Starting MRR - Churned MRR - Contraction MRR) ÷ Starting MRR
```

**Benchmarks:**
- Below 80%: Poor — losing too much revenue
- 80-90%: Average
- 90-100%: Excellent — strong retention

### Net Revenue Retention (NRR)

Revenue retained including expansion:

```
NRR = (Starting MRR - Churned MRR - Contraction MRR + Expansion MRR) ÷ Starting MRR
```

**Benchmarks:**
- Below 100%: Shrinking revenue from existing customers
- 100-120%: Healthy growth from existing base
- 120%+: Excellent — strong expansion revenue

### Logo Retention Rate

Simple customer count retention:

```
Logo Retention = (Customers at End - New Customers) ÷ Customers at Start
```

## Common Cohort Insights

### Onboarding Cohorts
Group by onboarding completion:
- Completed onboarding: 85% retention at Month 3
- Didn't complete: 40% retention at Month 3

**Action:** Invest in onboarding completion

### Plan Cohorts
Group by pricing plan:
- Free: 30% retention at Month 6
- Starter: 60% retention at Month 6
- Pro: 80% retention at Month 6

**Action:** Upsell free users to Starter faster

### Source Cohorts
Group by acquisition channel:
- Organic: 75% retention at Month 6
- Paid: 55% retention at Month 6
- Referral: 85% retention at Month 6

**Action:** Double down on referrals and organic

## How to Improve Cohort Retention

### 1. Fix Onboarding (First 7 Days)
The first week determines everything:
- Send welcome email immediately
- Guide to "aha moment" in under 5 minutes
- Follow up on Day 3, 7, 14
- Offer live onboarding for high-value users

### 2. Identify Churn Signals
Track leading indicators:
- Login frequency decreasing
- Feature usage dropping
- Support tickets increasing
- NPS score declining

### 3. Build Retention Loops
Features that create habits:
- Daily/weekly email summaries
- Notifications and alerts
- Team collaboration
- Integrations with daily tools

### 4. Segment and Personalize
Different customers need different approaches:
- Power users → advanced features
- Struggling users → support outreach
- At-risk users → retention campaigns
