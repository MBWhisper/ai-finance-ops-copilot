You can't manage what you don't measure. These 10 financial metrics are the foundation of every successful SaaS business — and the ones investors look at first.

## 1. Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)

**What it is:** Normalized monthly revenue from active subscriptions.

```
MRR = Number of Paying Customers × Average Revenue Per Account
```

**Why it matters:** The single most important growth metric for SaaS.

**Benchmarks:**
- $0-$10K: Pre-product-market fit
- $10K-$50K: Early traction
- $50K-$500K: Growth stage
- $500K+: Scale stage

## 2. Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR)

**What it is:** MRR × 12. The annualized version of MRR.

```
ARR = MRR × 12
```

**Why it matters:** Used for valuation and long-term planning.

**Valuation formula:** Most SaaS companies are valued at 5-15x ARR.

## 3. MRR Growth Rate

**What it is:** Month-over-month growth in MRR.

```
MRR Growth Rate = (Current MRR - Previous MRR) ÷ Previous MRR × 100
```

**Benchmarks:**
- Below 5%: Slow growth
- 5-10%: Healthy
- 10-20%: Fast growth
- 20%+: Exceptional (rare)

## 4. Churn Rate

**What it is:** Percentage of customers or revenue lost per month.

```
Customer Churn = Customers Lost ÷ Starting Customers × 100
Revenue Churn = Revenue Lost ÷ Starting MRR × 100
```

**Benchmarks:**
- Below 3%: Excellent
- 3-5%: Average
- 5-7%: Needs improvement
- Above 7%: Critical

## 5. Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)

**What it is:** Total revenue a customer generates over their lifetime.

```
LTV = ARPA ÷ Monthly Churn Rate
```

**Why it matters:** Determines how much you can spend on acquisition.

## 6. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

**What it is:** Total cost to acquire one new customer.

```
CAC = Total Sales & Marketing Spend ÷ New Customers
```

**The golden ratio:** LTV:CAC should be 3:1 or higher.

## 7. Net Revenue Retention (NRR)

**What it is:** Revenue retained from existing customers including expansion.

```
NRR = (Starting MRR - Churn - Contraction + Expansion) ÷ Starting MRR × 100
```

**Benchmarks:**
- Below 100%: Shrinking
- 100-120%: Healthy
- 120%+: Excellent (negative net churn)

## 8. Burn Rate

**What it is:** How fast you're spending cash.

```
Net Burn = Monthly Expenses - Monthly Revenue
```

**Why it matters:** Determines your runway (how long until you run out of money).

## 9. Runway

**What it is:** How many months until cash runs out.

```
Runway = Cash Balance ÷ Net Burn Rate
```

**Target:** Always maintain 18+ months of runway.

## 10. Gross Margin

**What it is:** Revenue minus cost of goods sold (COGS).

```
Gross Margin = (Revenue - COGS) ÷ Revenue × 100
```

**Benchmarks:**
- Below 60%: Low (infrastructure-heavy)
- 60-70%: Average
- 70-80%: Good
- 80%+: Excellent (typical SaaS)
